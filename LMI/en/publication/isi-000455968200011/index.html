Photogeneration Of Manganese(Iii) From Luminescent Manganese(Ii) Complexes With Thiacalixarene Ligands: Synthesis, Structures And Photophysical Properties

Niall O’Toole, Constance Lecourt, Yan Suffren, Andreas Hauser, Lhoussain Khrouz, Erwann Jeanneau, Arnaud Brioude, Dominique Luneau, Cedric Desroches
January 2019
DOI

Abstract

The Photophysical Properties Of The Compound(Thiaso(2))(Mn-Ii)(2)(Dmf)(4)(H2O)(2), Thiaso(2) =P-Tert-Butylsulfonylcalix[4]Arene, Are Presented And Compared To Theones Of [(Thiaso(2))(2)(Mn-Ii)(4)F]K (1). The Orange Luminescence Of 2Is Attributed, As For 1, To The Mn-Ii Centred T-4(1)->(6)A(1) Transitionand Shows, For This Type Of Complex, The Weak Influence Of The Mn2+Coordination And Thiaso(2) Conformation On This Luminescence, Thetemperature And Pressure Dependence And Quenching By Molecular Dioxygenof Which Are Reported For 2. The Latter Is Attributed To Energy Transferfrom The T-4(1) State Exciting Dioxygen To Its (1)Sigma(+)(G) State Andis Responsible For The Photosynthesis Of The[(Thiaso(2))(Mn-Iii)(Dmf)(2)]Na (3) Complex In Dmf Solution From 1 Or2. This Reaction Was Studied By Uv/Visible And Epr Spectroscopy. Themolecular Structure And Epr Spectroscopy Of 3 Are Also Presented.
